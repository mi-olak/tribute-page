Fela Anikulapo Kuti

Abami Eda!

Here is a time line of Fela's life:

Background Information

  • Birth name: Olufela Olusegun Oludotun Ransome-Kuti
  • Born: 15 October 1938
  • Hometown: Abeokuta, Nigeria
  • Died: August 1997 (aged 58)
  • Occupation: Singer-songwriter, musician, activist
  • Instruments: Saxophone, vocals, keyboards, trumpet, guitar, drums
1938
He was born into the Ransome-Kuti family, an upper-middle-class Nigerian family, in Abeokuta, capital of Ogun State
1958
he was sent to London to study medicine but decided to study music instead at the Trinity College of Music
1966
Fela married his first wife, Remilekun Taylor, with whom he had three children (Femi, Yeni, and Sola)
1969
Fela took the band to the United States and spent ten months in Los Angeles. While there, he discovered the Black Power movement through Sandra Smith
1970
Kuti and his band returned to Nigeria, the group was renamed (the) Africa '70 as lyrical themes changed from love to social issues.
1977
Fela and Africa 70 released the album Zombie, which heavily criticized Nigerian soldiers, and used the zombie metaphor to describe the Nigerian military's methods. The album was a massive success and infuriated the government, who raided the Kalakuta Republic with 1,000 soldiers.
1978
He married 27 women
1984
Muhammadu Buhari's government, of which Kuti was a vocal opponent, jailed him on a charge of currency smuggling
1989
Fela and Egypt 80 released the anti-apartheid album Beasts of No Nation that depicted U.S. President Ronald Reagan, UK Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, and South African State President Pieter Willem Botha on its cover.
1990s
fela's album output slowed in the 1990s, and eventually, he ceased releasing albums altogether.

3, August 1997

Kuti's brother Olikoye Ransome-Kuti, already a prominent AIDS activist and former Minister of Health, announced that Kuti had died on the previous day from complications related to AIDS.

Kuti is remembered as an influential icon who voiced his opinions on matters that affected the nation through his music. Since 1998, the Felabration festival, an idea pioneered by his daughter Yeni Kuti, is held each year at at the New Afrika Shrine to celebrate the life of this music legend and his birthday. Since Kuti's death in 1997, there has been a revival of his influence in music and popular culture.
